It Starts With Nothing

A blank screen.
No instructions. No pressure. No expectations.

Nothing but empty space staring back at you.

You tap the screen and suddenly there's this crooked line - not what you expected, but it's a start. It's not perfect, but hey, it's yours.
That’s how it begins.

You Don’t Need to Be Good

There’s no right way to draw here.

No rules about proportions. No need for perfect shapes. Move your finger around, pick whatever color feels right, and see what happens.

You try something small. Maybe a word. Maybe a shape. Maybe nothing that makes sense.
Honestly? Honestly, that weird little scribble I made was way more fun than I thought it'd be.

We kept it simple - just the basics you actually need.

We kept it really simple because, let's be real, nobody wants to hunt through tons of menus when you just wanna doodle something quick.
What you can do:

  • Choose colors from a bright palette

  • Draw freely on the canvas

  • Switch tools instantly

  • Erase, redraw, try again

No fancy features to confuse you - just draw.

It’s Not About the Result

At some point, you stop thinking about what you’re making.

You just draw.

Lines turn into shapes. Shapes turn into something unexpected. And even if it doesn’t look “finished,” it still feels complete.
Whatever you make is yours - could be a total disaster or something weird, but who cares?

Just Make the First Line

You don’t need a plan.
You don’t need a goal.
Just start.
